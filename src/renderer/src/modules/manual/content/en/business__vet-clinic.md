# Veterinary Clinic

This business type's screens are in English only, regardless of your language setting elsewhere in Sarang.

## The shared service foundation

Every service-based business type in Sarang — including Veterinary Clinic — starts from the same four building blocks: **Appointments** (booking and scheduling visits), a **Service Catalog** (the list of consultations, procedures, and their prices), **Provider Schedules** (which vet is available when), and an automatic **Notification Queue** that handles reminders (like the vaccination reminders below) without you having to send them by hand. The rest of this chapter covers what's specific to a vet clinic.

## Patients

Open **Patients** from the sidebar to see every animal registered at your clinic, not the human owners. Each patient card shows the species (with an emoji marker for Dog/Cat/Bird/Rabbit/Reptile/Other), breed, gender, weight, and a vaccination-status badge (Up to Date / Due Soon / Overdue / No Records). Filter by species, search by patient or owner name, or switch to the **Archived** view for patients no longer active.

Click **Add Patient** to register a new one — name, species, breed, date of birth, gender, color/markings, microchip ID, an optional linked owner (searched from your existing Customers, or left as a walk-in), and free-text notes for allergies or chronic conditions.

A banner at the top of the Patients list surfaces **Upcoming Vaccinations** due in the next 30 days across all patients, so nothing gets missed.

## Patient profile

Opening a patient takes you to three tabs:

- **Overview** — the patient's details, linked owner card, and a **Weight History** log. Add a new weigh-in at any time; once there are two or more entries, a small trend chart plots weight over time.
- **Vaccinations** — every vaccination record (vaccine name, type, batch number, manufacturer, administered date, next due date, administering vet). Each record shows a status badge (Overdue / Due in Xd / Up to date). From here you can **queue a WhatsApp reminder** for an upcoming due date (skipped automatically if the owner has no phone number on file), or **print a vaccination certificate**.
- **Appointments** — the patient's full visit history with status (Scheduled, Confirmed, In Progress, Completed, Cancelled, No-show).

Editing a patient also lets you **archive** it (hides it from the active list without deleting history) and restore it later.

## Vaccination certificates

Printing a vaccination certificate produces a formal one-page document with the clinic's letterhead, patient and vaccine details, and a disclaimer that it is a convenience document generated by Sarang, not a validated veterinary record — always verify details before relying on it clinically.

## Consultation notes

When booking an appointment, pick the specific **patient (pet)** it's for. Once the visit happens, open **Clinical Notes** to record a real consultation — vitals, findings, and plan — the same structured note-taking every clinical vertical in Sarang shares. The note is prefilled with the pet's own name and age (not the owner's), and shows the pet's species, breed, sex, and owner right alongside it for quick context.

Vitals are checked against **normal ranges** that account for the patient's species — a dog's normal temperature and pulse range genuinely differ from a cat's or a human's, and Sarang evaluates each reading against the right one automatically.
