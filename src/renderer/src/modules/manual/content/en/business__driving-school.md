# Driving School

Driving School is one of Sarang's 24 specific service-business templates. Like every business type in that group, this business type's screens are in English only, regardless of your language setting elsewhere in Sarang.

Every service-business template shares the same foundation: **Appointments** for booking, a **Service Catalog**, **Provider Schedule** for staff working hours, and a background **Notification Queue** for reminders. Driving School adds its own dedicated screen — with five tabs — for the parts of running a driving school that don't fit a generic appointment: learner profiles, driving sessions, vehicles, tests, and packages.

## Learners

Pick any existing customer from the search list on the left to open their **learner profile** on the right: license class (LMV, HMV, two-wheeler, or a combination), a preferred vehicle class, DL application number, learner's-license number and issue date, and permanent-license number and issue date once they've passed. This is the compliance record a driving school needs to track a learner's progress from learner's permit to full license.

## Driving Sessions

Each individual driving lesson is scheduled with a learner, an instructor, an active vehicle, a date/time, a duration, and an optional pickup point. A session's status moves through **Scheduled → Completed** (or **No Show**). Once completed, you can either:

- enter a **session fee** and generate a one-off invoice for just that lesson, or
- redeem it against a **package** the learner has already bought (see Packages below), in which case there's no separate invoice — it's marked "Via package" instead.

The Sessions tab filters by Today, All, Scheduled, or Completed.

## Vehicles

The school's own instructional-vehicle fleet: registration number, make/model, vehicle class (LMV, two-wheeler, HMV), an assigned instructor, and a status (Active, Maintenance, Retired). Only vehicles marked Active can be picked when scheduling a new session.

## Tests

Tracks a learner's actual test bookings — learner's-license test or driving test — with a test date, test center, and a result: Pending, Passed, or Failed, with an optional retest date if they don't pass the first time.

## Packages

A driving school's most common billing pattern is selling a bundle of N lessons up front rather than billing lesson-by-lesson. **Packages** has two parts:

- **Package Catalog** — define a package's name, total number of sessions, price, and which vehicle class it applies to.
- **Learner Enrollments** — enroll a learner into a package, track sessions used against the total, and generate the package's invoice once (a package is billed as a whole, not per session). Each session scheduled against that enrollment is deducted from its remaining count automatically instead of needing its own fee or invoice.
