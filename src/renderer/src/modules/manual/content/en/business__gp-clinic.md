# GP Clinic

This business type's screens are in English only, regardless of your language setting elsewhere in Sarang.

## The shared service foundation

Every service-based business type in Sarang — including GP Clinic — starts from the same four building blocks: **Appointments** (booking and scheduling visits), a **Service Catalog** (the list of consultations and their prices), **Provider Schedules** (which doctor is available when), and an automatic **Notification Queue** that handles reminders without you having to send them by hand. The rest of this chapter covers what's specific to a GP clinic: consultation notes and a walk-in token queue.

## Consultation Notes (Visit Notes)

Opening an appointment's **Consultation Note** gives you a structured, SOAP-format clinical note:

- **Patient Information** — name, age, chief complaint.
- **S — Subjective**: what the patient reports (history, symptoms, onset).
- **Vitals**: BP (systolic/diastolic), pulse, temperature, height, weight — each field is automatically flagged (Normal / Low / High) against a saved normal-range reference once you save, so out-of-range readings stand out immediately.
- **O — Objective**: examination findings.
- **A — Assessment**: diagnosis / clinical impression.
- **P — Plan**: treatment plan, medications, investigations ordered.
- **Follow-up**: an optional follow-up date and instructions.

Click **Save Note** as you go, then **Finalize** when the consultation is complete. A finalized note becomes read-only (shown with a lock badge) — this protects the clinical record from being altered after the fact. You can **Print Summary** at any time to hand the patient (or keep for your files) a formatted visit summary, which carries a clear disclaimer that it's a convenience document generated by Sarang, not a validated medical record — always verify before clinical use.

**Prescription.** Add a real prescription as its own itemized list — drug name, dosage, frequency, duration, and instructions, one row per medicine — separate from the free-text Plan field above. **Print Prescription** produces a proper ℞ document with the itemized drug table (unlike the general visit summary, this one is meant to serve as an actual prescription, so it doesn't carry the "not a validated record" disclaimer — it just needs your signature/stamp to be valid).

**Vitals Trend.** Once a patient has two or more visits with recorded vitals, a trend chart appears showing how a chosen metric (BP, pulse, temperature, or weight) has moved over time — pick which metric to plot from the chip row above the chart.

**Referral letters.** Using the existing "Refer to Another Provider" action creates a real referral; once one exists, **Print Referral Letter** produces a formal letter addressed to the referred-to doctor with the reason for referral — a genuinely different document from the full consultation summary, built for handing to the patient to take to the specialist.

## Token Queue

The **Token Queue** screen manages same-day walk-in patients without needing a pre-booked appointment. It shows:

- A large **Now Serving** display of the current token number and patient name.
- Count chips for Waiting / Called / Seen / Skipped.
- **Add Walk-in** to issue a new token (patient name, age, gender, phone, notes).
- **Call Next** to call the next waiting token.

Each token in the list can be called, marked seen, skipped, or reset back to waiting — the queue re-sorts itself into "Currently Called," "Waiting," and "Completed" sections automatically. This is entirely separate from the pre-booked Appointments list — it's built for the reality of patients who simply walk in and wait their turn.
