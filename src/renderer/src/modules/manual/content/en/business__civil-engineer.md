# Civil Engineer

This business type's screens are in English only, regardless of your language setting elsewhere in Sarang.

## The shared service foundation

Every service-based business type in Sarang — including Civil Engineer — starts from the same four building blocks: **Appointments** (booking client meetings), a **Service Catalog** (the list of services and their prices), **Provider Schedules** (which team member is available when), and an automatic **Notification Queue** that handles reminders without you having to send them by hand. The rest of this chapter covers what's specific to a civil engineering practice: a lead pipeline, project management, time tracking, and the site visit log.

## Leads

**Leads** is a Kanban-style pipeline of prospective clients: Open → Contacted → Proposal → Won → Lost. Drag a lead card between columns to update its status, or add a new lead with name, contact details, company, source (Referral, Website, Walk-In, Social, Cold Call, Other), estimated value, and an assigned team member.

## Projects

**Service Projects** track each client engagement from contract to completion — project name, type, stage, status (Active / On Hold / Completed / Cancelled), total contract value, start and expected-end dates, and an assigned team member. Each project can carry **milestones** — named deliverables with their own amount and due date — and once a milestone is complete, generate an invoice for it directly from the project.

## Time Entries

Log billable hours against a project from the standalone **Time Tracking** screen — date, staff, description, hours, rate, and computed amount — filterable by staff, project, date range, and billed status. Select unbilled entries and **Generate Invoice** to bill the client directly.

## Site Visit Log

The **Site Visit Log** is the real, day-to-day differentiator for a civil engineering practice: for each project, record every visit to the site — visit date, visit type (Survey, Inspection, Progress Check, Handover), weather conditions, and your findings as free text. Each visit records who recorded it, and you can attach files (photos, survey documents, inspection reports) to each visit. This builds a running, project-by-project record of exactly what was observed on site and when — distinct from an architect's drawing-issue workflow, and built around the survey/inspection/progress-check reality of civil engineering work.

Click **Use Current Location** on a visit to capture its real GPS coordinates (with an accuracy reading shown alongside), or enter latitude/longitude manually if you're logging a visit after the fact.

Each visit also has a **Tests** panel for structured material test results — test type, material, measured value, unit, and the required minimum. Sarang automatically marks a test Pass or Fail once both the measured value and required minimum are entered; without a required minimum it's recorded as Pending rather than guessed.
